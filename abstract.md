**Abstract:** *This project presents life-clean, a production-grade 10-phase computational biology architecture designed to discover tissue-specific gene therapy delivery vehicles for safe human cellular rejuvenation. To mitigate the oncogenic risk associated with prolonged Oct4/Sox2/Klf4 (OSK) expression, the pipeline integrates an automated Bayesian optimization loop that enforces a strict transient expression threshold modeled on active 2026 clinical benchmarks (Life Biosciences' ER-100 trial). The framework handles processing across billion-scale candidate spaces by coupling structural protein language modeling (ESM-2) and dynamic cheminformatics (RDKit) with a live data ingestion bridge that streams functional screening metrics directly from open-source repositories (Harvard's AAV Fitness Landscape, LNPDB, and Fit4Function). The system automates downstream wet-lab translation by exporting customized microfluidic manufacturing instructions (Opentrons protocols) alongside a fully budgeted contract research organization (CRO) validation roadmap, demonstrating how software-driven pipelines can scale to accelerate the arrival of next-generation therapeutics.*
